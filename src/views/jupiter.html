Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with...

Stats

Diameter: 139,822 km

Mass: 1.898 × 10^27 kg

Orbital period: 11.86 years

Distance from sun: 778.5 million km

Features

Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a giant storm that has been raging for at least 400 years.

It has a faint ring system made up of dust particles.

Moons

Jupiter has a vast number of moons, with over 79 confirmed moons.

Io: Io is the most volcanically active body in the Solar System.

Europa: Europa has a subsurface ocean that could potentially harbor life.

Ganymede: Ganymede is the largest moon in the Solar System.

Callisto: Callisto has the most heavily cratered surface of any moon in the Solar System.

Explorations

Jupiter has been visited by several spacecraft, including...

Pioneer 10: Pioneer 10 was the first spacecraft to fly by Jupiter in 1973.

Voyager 1 & 2: The Voyager probes provided detailed images and data of Jupiter and its moons.

Galileo: The Galileo spacecraft orbited Jupiter for eight years, studying its atmosphere, moons, and magnetosphere.

Juno: Launched in 2011, Juno has been studying Jupiter's composition, gravity field, magnetic field, and polar magnetosphere.

Potential for Life

Jupiter is not thought to have conditions suitable for life as we know it.

Fun Facts

Jupiter's mass is 2.5 times that of all the other planets in the Solar System combined.

Jupiter's magnetic field is 14 times stronger than Earth's.
