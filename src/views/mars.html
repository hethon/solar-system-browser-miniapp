Mars | መሪህ

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second smallest planet in the Solar System.

Stats

Diameter: 6,779 km

Mass: 6.39 × 10^23 kg

Orbital period: 687 days

Distance from sun: 227.9 million km

Features

Mars has a thin atmosphere primarily composed of carbon dioxide.

It has distinct surface features including valleys, volcanoes, and polar ice caps.

Moons

Mars has two natural satellites, Phobos and Deimos.

Phobos: Phobos is the larger and closer of Mars' moons.

Deimos: Deimos is the smaller and outermost of Mars' moons.

Explorations

Mars has been extensively explored by both robotic spacecraft and orbiters.

Mars Rovers: Rovers like Spirit, Opportunity, and Curiosity have explored the Martian surface, studying its geology and searching for signs of past life.

Mars Orbiters: Orbiters like Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter and MAVEN have provided detailed images and data of Mars' surface, atmosphere, and climate.

Future Missions: Planned missions, including the Mars Perseverance rover and future crewed missions, aim to further explore Mars and prepare for potential human settlement.

Potential for Life

Mars is currently inhospitable to life as we know it, but evidence suggests it may have had conditions suitable for life in the past.

Future exploration aims to search for signs of past or present life on Mars.

Fun Facts

Mars has the tallest volcano and the largest canyon in the Solar System, Olympus Mons and Valles Marineris, respectively.

Mars' surface is covered in iron oxide, giving it a reddish appearance and earning it the nickname 'the Red Planet.'
