Mercury | ዐጣርድ

Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System and the closest to the Sun.

Stats

Diameter: 4,880 km

Mass: 3.301 × 10^23 kg

Orbital period: 88 days

Distance from sun: 57.9 million km

Features

Mercury has a thin atmosphere composed mainly of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium.

Its surface is heavily cratered and resembles Earth's moon.

Moons

Mercury does not have any moons.

Explorations

Mercury has been visited by spacecraft from Earth.

Mariner 10: Mariner 10 was the first spacecraft to visit Mercury in 1974. It provided the first close-up images of the planet.

MESSENGER: MESSENGER (MErcury Surface, Space ENvironment, GEochemistry, and Ranging) was a NASA spacecraft that orbited Mercury from 2011 to 2015, providing detailed observations of its surface, magnetic field, and atmosphere.

Potential for Life

Mercury is too hot and lacks the necessary conditions to support life as we know it.

Its surface experiences extreme temperature variations, ranging from about -180°C (-290°F) at night to 430°C (800°F) during the day.

Fun Facts

Mercury has the shortest year of all the planets in the Solar System, completing an orbit around the Sun in just 88 Earth days.

It also has the slowest rotation period, taking about 59 Earth days to rotate once on its axis.
