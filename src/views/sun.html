The Sun is the star at the center of the Solar System.

Stats

Diameter: 1.39 million km

Mass: 1.989 × 10^30 kg

Age: Approximately 4.6 billion years

Surface temperature: Approximately 5,500°C (9,932°F)

Features

The Sun is composed mainly of hydrogen (about 74%) and helium (about 24%) with small amounts of heavier elements.

It generates energy through nuclear fusion in its core, converting hydrogen into helium and releasing vast amounts of energy in the process.

Moons

The Sun does not have any moons.

Explorations

The Sun has been studied extensively using telescopes and spacecraft.

Solar Observatories: Ground-based observatories and space-based telescopes like the Solar and Heliospheric Observatory (SOHO) provide continuous observations of the Sun's activity.

Parker Solar Probe: Launched in 2018, the Parker Solar Probe is a NASA spacecraft that is studying the Sun's outer atmosphere and solar wind.

Potential for Life

The Sun is essential for life on Earth, providing heat and light that sustain almost all living organisms.

However, direct exposure to the Sun's intense radiation can be harmful to living organisms and requires protection.

Fun Facts

The Sun is so massive that it contains more than 99.8% of the total mass of the Solar System.

It takes about 8 minutes and 20 seconds for light from the Sun to reach Earth.
