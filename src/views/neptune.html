Neptune | ኔፕቱን

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun in the Solar System.

Stats

Diameter: 49,244 km

Mass: 1.024 × 10^26 kg

Orbital period: 164.8 years

Distance from sun: 4.5 billion km

Features

Neptune is a gas giant with a thick atmosphere primarily composed of hydrogen, helium, and trace amounts of methane.

It has the strongest winds in the Solar System, with speeds reaching up to 2,100 km/h (1,300 mph).

Moons

Neptune has 14 known moons, with the largest being Triton.

Triton: Triton is the only large moon in the Solar System that orbits in the opposite direction of its planet's rotation.

Nereid: Nereid is Neptune's third-largest moon and has a highly elliptical orbit.

Proteus: Proteus is one of the largest irregularly shaped moons in the Solar System.

Explorations

Neptune has been visited by only one spacecraft to date.

Voyager 2: Voyager 2 passed by Neptune in 1989, providing the first close-up images and data of the planet and its moons.

Potential for Life

Neptune is a gas giant and lacks a solid surface, making it inhospitable to life as we know it.

Its extreme cold temperatures and high atmospheric pressure would pose significant challenges for any potential life forms.

Fun Facts

Neptune was the first planet to be discovered through mathematical predictions rather than direct observation.

It has a faint ring system and a large dark spot in its atmosphere similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot.
